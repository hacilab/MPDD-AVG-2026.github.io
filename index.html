Overview

Call for Participation

Depression is a pervasive mental health disorder with a profound impact on global wellbeing, affecting millions and significantly hindering quality of life, productivity, and overall health. The WHO categorizes depression as a leading cause of disability, underscoring its substantial societal and economic burden.

Existing approaches predominantly rely on conversational or interview-based modalities with limited age diversity, while ambulatory behavioral signatures such as gait characteristics — though recognized as important clinical indicators of psychomotor symptoms — remain largely unexplored in computational frameworks.

MPDD-AVG bridges these gaps by uniquely integrating semi-structured interview behavioral data with continuous gait monitoring from wearable sensors, enabling holistic assessment spanning cognitive-linguistic, affective-paralinguistic, and psychomotor domains. This is an updated version of MPDD2025 @ ACM MM 2025.

Critically, we provide raw individual difference annotations — including Big Five-10 personality dimensions, demographic variables, and health condition labels — explicitly encouraging participants to develop innovative personality-conditioned modeling strategies.

Please cite: Fu, C., Fu, Z., Zhang, Q., Kuang, X., Dong, J., Su, K., ... & Ishiguro, H. (2025, October). The First MPDD Challenge: Multimodal Personality-aware Depression Detection. In Proceedings of the 33rd ACM International Conference on Multimedia (pp. 13924–13929).
🎙️

Audio-Visual Interview

Semi-structured interviews capturing cognitive-linguistic and affective-paralinguistic cues from both young adults and elderly participants.

🚶

Gait Analysis via IMU

Wearable IMU sensors capturing walking speed, stride regularity, and postural dynamics as psychomotor depression indicators.

🧠

Personality-Aware Modeling

Raw Big Five-10 annotations with demographic and health condition data to model inter-individual heterogeneity.

👥

Age-Diverse Population

110 young adults (academic stress) and 110 elderly participants (late-life depression, chronic conditions).

Tracks

Track Setting & Evaluation

Two age-specific datasets, each featuring three complementary sub-tracks exploring different combinations of behavioral modalities and personality modeling.

MPDD-Young Dataset

Focuses on 110 college students, investigating how academic stress, social environment, and personality traits contribute to depression in young adults.

Audiovisual recordings from semi-structured interviews, gait data from wearable IMU sensors, Big Five-10 personality labels, and demographic information.

110 Young Adults PHQ-9 Scores Big Five-10 IMU Gait Demographics

Classification Tasks

Binary: Normal / Depressed

Ternary: Normal / Mildly Depressed / Severely Depressed

80% training + validation  ·  20% testing  ·  Standardized splits provided

Sub-track 1
A-V+P
Audio-Visual with Personality Modeling. Interview acoustic and visual features conditioned on Big Five-10 personality traits.
Sub-track 2
A-V-G+P
Audio-Visual-Gait with Personality. Full multimodal fusion of conversational cues, ambulatory patterns, and personality information.
Sub-track 3
G+P
Gait with Personality Factors. Psychomotor depression detection from IMU signals conditioned on personality traits.

MPDD-Elderly Dataset

Examines depression in 110 older adults, exploring how chronic illnesses, living conditions, and personality traits influence late-life depression manifestation.

Audiovisual interviews, gait monitoring, Big Five-10 personality labels, health condition annotations (endocrine, circulatory, nervous system), and detailed demographic information.

110 Older Adults PHQ-9 Scores Big Five-10 Disease Labels IMU Gait

Classification Tasks

Binary: Normal / Depressed

Ternary: Normal / Mildly Depressed / Severely Depressed

Quinary: Normal / Mild / Moderate / Moderately Severe / Severe

Includes chronic disease annotations: endocrine · circulatory · nervous system

Sub-track 1
A-V+P
Audio-Visual with Personality Modeling. Interview features with personality-conditioned late-life depression assessment.
Sub-track 2
A-V-G+P
Audio-Visual-Gait with Personality. Holistic elderly depression detection spanning cognitive, affective, and psychomotor domains.
Sub-track 3
G+P
Gait with Personality Factors. Ambulatory pattern analysis for elderly depression with chronic condition awareness.

Evaluation

Evaluation Metrics

Comprehensive metrics assessing model performance across classification and regression tasks, accounting for class imbalance and clinical relevance.

Classification Metrics

AccuracyACC
Macro F1-ScoreMacro-F1
Cohen's Kappaκ

Regression Metrics

Root Mean Squared ErrorRMSE
Mean Absolute ErrorMAE
Concordance Correlation CoefficientCCC
Track-Level Composite Score
Scoretrack = α · Macro-F1 + β · CCC + γ · κ
where α + β + γ = 1, balancing severity classification, continuous PHQ-9 prediction, and diagnostic consistency.
